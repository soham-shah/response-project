A quick background on the impetus behind this project

Conceptually, people know that a lot of their information is online. However, I believe that they fail to realize how much there really is. Conceptually, people know that a lot of their information is online. However, I believe that they fail to realize how much there really is.

Without knowing what personal information is publically accessible online, people cannot make informed decisions about what data they would like others to have access to.

The following is a guide to finding information about yourself online and some resources on how you can get that information removed.

Step 1: start with Search

Each search engine will have different search results so try entering your name in the following and see what pops up.

  • Google
  • Bing
  • Yahoo
  • Duck Duck Go

Step 2: Narrow Your search

Keywords

Try some keywords associated with yourself, like what school you went to, or where you work.

Close Associations

Try searching your name in association with some friends or family in order to narrow your search further

Mispellings

If there is a different popular spelling of your name, try that in your search as well. For example, "Cong" and "Kong"

Search Modifiers

  • Remove terms
    • Use the "-" followed by any word to remove any results with that word from the search result. ex: "Johnny Depp -actor"
  • Force include
    • Use quotation marks around a work to force all results to have that word. ex "Bill "Boulder""
  • Site Specific Search
    • Use the "site:" modifier to search specific sites on the internet. ex: "Bill site:denverpost.com"

Step 3: Social Networks

People know that everyone can see the information you post on social networks. But the question is, what information do you post?

There are websites which will allow others to search across all social media platforms and provide that information to users Let's see what they turn up

Special Databases

Often there are specialized databases that don't show up in traditional search results that we need to check

News

Make sure to check the Google News results seperately. The Search modifier information still apples

Advanced Tips

Reverse image search

Once you grabbed a photo of yourself with a decently high resolution, run it through a reverse image search engine to see what it can find.

Some engines are

  • Tineye
  • Google Images

Options Available to you

If you live in the EU

Great! You're protected under the Right to be Forgotten. You can contact Google and the other search engines, and ask for your private information to be removed fromt their search results.

If you live in the US

You're out of luck. You need to contact each website that's listed on your search results individually and ask for your information to be removed.

Other things you can do

  • Clean up your social media profiles.
    • While your photos may already be out there on the internet, you can certainly make them harder to find by removing all the old stuff from your public social media profiles.
