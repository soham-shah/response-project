The Eye of Sauron

Background

Some thoughts on the impetus behind this project

Conceptually, people know that a lot of their information is online. However, I believe that they don't know specifics, which makes it hard for them to understand why it's such a big issue.

Without knowing what personal information of theirs is publically accessible online, people cannot make informed decisions about what data they would like others to have access to.

According to Career Builder's survey of hiring managers

60% of employers shared that they use social media sites to research potential employees. This is just one example of

The following is a guide to finding information about yourself online and some resources on how you can get that information removed.

The Goal behind the guide is to help inform you about what people online can find out about you, and provide some resources on how to get that taken down.

Step 0: Before you start:

While there is a lot of data about you online, you should pay special attention to see if there is anything that you don't want others to see

Types of Data

Some things that are available about you include:

  • Your address
  • Your phone number
  • People you're associated with
  • News coverage about you
  • Arrest records
  • Unflattering images of you
  • And so on...

The Goal

The Goal behind this website is to provide a resource to help you locate that information and some resources on how to get it taken down

As you're going through each of the steps outlined later, make sure that you're tracking what you find and saving all the links so you can go back later to remove them.

Step 1: start with Search

Each search engine will have different search results so try entering your name in the following and see what pops up.

  • Google
  • Bing
  • Yahoo
  • Duck Duck Go

Step 2: Narrow Your search

The Goal

The goal of this is to start filtering our noise and unnecessary results to really find some important information and where it's hidden. There are many paid companies that do this automatically, but we want to figure out what people can surface for free if they use the right query.

Keywords

Try some keywords associated with yourself, like what school you went to, the company you work at, passions of yours, the city you live in, etc.

Close Associations

Try searching your name in association with some friends or family in order to narrow your search further

Misspellings

If there is a different popular spelling of your name, try that in your search as well. For example, "Cong" and "Kong"

Nicknames

If you go by a nickname, make sure to try both combinations of your name

Maiden Name

Same as above, try both your married name and your maiden name.

Search Modifiers

Search modifiers are powerful ways of narrowing your search results by structuring your search in a specific way

  • Remove terms
    • Use the "-" followed by any word to remove any results with that word from the search result. ex: "Johnny Depp -actor"
  • Force include
    • Use quotation marks around a work to force all results to have that word. ex "Bill "Boulder""

Search Modifiers Continued

  • Site Specific Search
    • Use the "site:" modifier to search specific sites on the internet. ex: "Bill site:colorado.edu"

Advanced Search

If the search modifiers are too confusing, try using the advanced search page for each search engine to narrow down your search

Step 4: People Search Engines

There are search engines online designed to specifically search for what public records on you are available. Let's see what they can find.

Tip: try using a couple which sound interesting. No need to try them all

Who Where

Like an online yellow pages, this allows you to find addresses and phone numbers of people

Pipl

One of the scariest people search I've found. It finds employment, usernames, dates of birth, news articles, and online posts

Spokeo

Can Find: age, address, relatives, locations where you have lived.

FullContact

Provide them an email address, and this service will fill in the rest of the information on what it knows for everyone in your address book. Great for business users, but also quite scary!

Intelius

Intelius provides phone numbers, addresses, names of relatives, and past employment data

Step 5: Special Databases

Often there are specialized databases that don't show up in traditional search results that we need to check

While this list is not comprehensive, it gives an idea of things you can look for

News

Make sure to check the Google News results separately. The Search modifier information still apples

News Continued

Often times local news records are not publically searchable

Try a search query like "John Doe site:denverpost.com" in order to search their web facing archive

Also try going to the local newspaper's website directly and running some searches there to see what you can unearth.

Public Records

Cities sometimes keep records about their citizens publically accessible. Mouse around on your city website and see if you can find out what information about you they're sharing to the world

Voter Records

Given a name, this will list your party affiliation, address, and where you are registered to vote

Birth Records

See if your birth record is publically accessible

Family Watchdog

Gives the names and locations of all known sex offenders, gives you pictures and profiles. If you're on here, you already know, but it is interesting to see what data on other people is available. Think about whether this is something you support or not.

Step 7: Clean up your online presence

Now, using everything that we've found, go through and think about what you would like taken off the internet and what you don't mind leaving online.

If you live in the EU

Great! You're protected under the Right to be Forgotten. You can contact Google and the other search engines, and ask for your private information to be removed from their search results.

Read the factsheet on the right to be forgotten ruling to understand your rights.

If you live in the US

There is no single overarching federal law giving citizens something similar to the right to be forgotten.

Read here for more information

Read on for tips you can follow to make it harder to find your information

Other things you can do

  • Clean up your social media profiles.
    • While your photos may already be out there on the internet, you can certainly make them harder to find by removing all the old stuff from your public social media profiles.
  • Delete old and unused social media accounts
  • Edit your privacy settings on the websites you turned up in your previous searches
  • Contact each website that's listed on your search results individually and ask for your information to be removed.

Fooling Search Algorithms

Search Engine algorithms operate using something called Page Rank which tries to surface the latest and most relevant content

You can trick these algorithms by creating new fresh content that you've chosen to represent you
