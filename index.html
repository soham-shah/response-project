The Eye of Sauron

Background

Some thoughts on the impetus behind this project

Conceptually, people know that a lot of their information is online. However, I believe that they fail to realize how much there really is.

Without knowing what personal information is publically accessible online, people cannot make informed decisions about what data they would like others to have access to.

The following is a guide to finding information about yourself online and some resources on how you can get that information removed.

Some things to keep in mind

While there is a lot of data about you online, we want to pay special attention to see if we can find stuff you don't want others to see

According to Career Builder's survey of hiring managers

60% of employers shared that they use social media sites to research potential employees.

Types of Data

Some things that are available about you include:

  • Your address
  • Your phone number
  • People you're associated with
  • News coverage about you
  • Arrest records
  • Unflattering images of you
  • And so on..

The Goal

The Goal behind this website is to provide a resource to help you locate that information and some resources on how to get it taken down

Step 1: start with Search

Each search engine will have different search results so try entering your name in the following and see what pops up.

  • Google
  • Bing
  • Yahoo
  • Duck Duck Go

Step 2: Narrow Your search

Keywords

Try some keywords associated with yourself, like what school you went to, or where you work.

Close Associations

Try searching your name in association with some friends or family in order to narrow your search further

Mispellings

If there is a different popular spelling of your name, try that in your search as well. For example, "Cong" and "Kong"

Search Modifiers

  • Remove terms
    • Use the "-" followed by any word to remove any results with that word from the search result. ex: "Johnny Depp -actor"
  • Force include
    • Use quotation marks around a work to force all results to have that word. ex "Bill "Boulder""
  • Site Specific Search
    • Use the "site:" modifier to search specific sites on the internet. ex: "Bill site:denverpost.com"

Step 4: People Search Engines

There are search engines online designed to specifically search for what public records on you are available.

Who Where

Like an online yellowpages, this allows you to find addresses and phone numbers of people

Birth Records

See if your birth record is publically accessible

Pipl

The scariest people search I've found. It finds employment, usernames, dates of birth, news articles, and online posts

Spokeo

Can Find: age, address, relatives, locations where you have lived.

FullContact

Provide them an email address and this service will fill in the rest of the information on what it knows for everyone in your address book. Great for business users, but also quite scary!

Intelius

Intelius provides phone numbers, addresses, names of relatives, and past employment data

Step 5: Special Databases

Often there are specialized databases that don't show up in traditional search results that we need to check

News

Make sure to check the Google News results seperately. The Search modifier information still apples

Often news websites are not publically searchable. Try searching the records of your local news websites to see what they have about you. You may not even know that you were in the news!

Public Records

Cities sometimes keep records about their citizens publically accessible. Mouse around on your city website and see if you can find out what information about you they're sharing to the world

Voter Records

Given a name, this will list your party affiliation, address, and where you are registered to vote

Family Watchdog

Gives the names and locations of all known sex offenders, gives you pictures and profiles.

The Smoking Gun

A searchable database of all mugshots.

Step 6: Advanced Tips

Reverse image search

Once you grabbed a photo of yourself with a decently high resolution, run it through a reverse image search engine to see what it can find.

Some engines are

  • Tineye
  • Google Images

Options Available to you

If you live in the EU

Great! You're protected under the Right to be Forgotten. You can contact Google and the other search engines, and ask for your private information to be removed fromt their search results.

If you live in the US

You're out of luck. You need to contact each website that's listed on your search results individually and ask for your information to be removed.

Other things you can do

  • Clean up your social media profiles.
    • While your photos may already be out there on the internet, you can certainly make them harder to find by removing all the old stuff from your public social media profiles.
